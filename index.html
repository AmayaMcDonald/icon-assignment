Race Disparities in Professional Sports

By Amaya McDonald – October 3, 2022

There are major racial disparities in American professional sports, which benefit from the talents of Black athletes. In the NFL’s 103 year history, none of the league’s 32 teams have ever been owned by a Black person. Among the league’s coaching staff, there are currently only three Black coaches in the entire NFL. This representation, or lack thereof, is inconsistent with the large percentage of Black athletes that make up the majority of NFL players.

As of 2020, 57.5% of NFL athletes identified as Black or African American. There are 1,696 athletes in the league this season.
There have been over 500 coaches in the history of the NFL. Only 24 of them are Black, the first being hired in 1989. This season, there are 3 Black head coaches in the league of 32 teams.
The NFL established the Rooney Rule in 2003 as a policy to require league teams to interview ethnic-minority candidates for head coaching and senior football operation jobs. Since 2003, only 27 of 127 head-coaching vacancies (21%) have been filled by minorities (as of January 2021).
This lack of diversity in leadership is not unique to the NFL, where there have only been two Black team presidents in history. The NBA, who’s Black athletes make up 74% of the league, currently has four Black team presidents in the league of 30 teams.
